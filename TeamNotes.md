Pervious team experiences: What worked being on team vs what did not.
Worked: Good communication in a timely manner.
Healthy ways to resolve disagreements.
Even work load (no 1/2 person(s) were doing all the work)
Taking time to meet to go over changes and what to keep.
Understanding that things evolve and our mindset needs to as well.
No control freaks, everyones idea is valid/ can be used.

Didn't work: Had a person state a previous group partner from a different class would literally wait until 2 hours before a project was due to provide feedback.
Disagreements that turned into shouting matches.
People not showing up at all.
1/ 2 people constantly doing all the work.
Never meeting and having issues with the ability to agree on work quality.
One person constantly trying to be the leader and shooting the ideas of others down.
An older group member who was stuck in a it's this way or no way mindset.

Team Health indicator: a good team health looks like healthy communication. This means brainstorming, executing and asking for feedback before presenting a final reult.
Setting time aside to meet: even if it is just for an 2-3 hoursout of the week taking the time to check in and see who needs better understanding on what as well as seeing if there is a personal issue that may keep another member from being able to be present.
Trusting other members to contribute and submit without needing too much oversight: if there is a general idea of what the goal is, in the evnt other members can not contribute the others should be able to fill in the gap on their part to reflect their quality of work.
Healthy conflict resolution: there are going to be disagreements and possibly even personal issues that may arise amongst team members, it is the teams responsibility to work through these issues and come to a common ground for the betterment of the team.

Psycological safety: all team members input is valued and will be treated with respect even if we do not agree with it. No will be made to feel mentally inept, called stupid, or told their ideas are dumb. That is grade school nonsense that needs to stay there. 
Take into consideration a persons mental state at all times. We are all dealing with things we do not talk about and sometimes they manifest as mood changes. Members need to have the emotional intelligence to identy thise signs and make sure they speak to that person with empathy and compassion when stating what they observe and ask what we can do to help.

Accountability: it is on the entire team to take responsibility when a project is not up to quality standards. That means there was a lack of proper oversight and contribution that would make it quality work. If there are team members not showing up costantly it needs to be called out. If someone is not pulling their weight, call it out. If we are not meeting up enough call it out. But also come to a solution to fix what is wrong. Accountability is not about be ing able to point the finger but to point out room for improvement.
